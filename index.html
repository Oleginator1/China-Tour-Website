About China

China, country of East Asia. It is the largest of all Asian countries. Occupying nearly the entire East Asian landmass, it covers approximately one-fourteenth of the land area of Earth, and it is almost as large as the whole of Europe. China is also one of the most populous countries in the world, rivaled only by India, which, according to United Nations estimates, surpassed it in population in 2023.
Within China’s boundaries exists a highly diverse and complex country. Its topography encompasses the highest and one of the lowest places on Earth, and its relief varies from nearly impenetrable mountainous terrain to vast coastal lowlands. Its climate ranges from extremely dry, desertlike conditions in the northwest to tropical monsoon in the southeast, and China has the greatest contrast in temperature between its northern and southern borders of any country in the world.

Chinese Culture

Explore the traditions, philosophies, and arts that have shaped China for over 5,000 years.

Festivals

The Spring Festival, Lantern Festival, and Mid-Autumn Festival bring families together with food, traditions, and fireworks.

Calligraphy & Arts

Brush calligraphy, Peking opera, and traditional painting remain key expressions of Chinese creativity and philosophy.

Martial Arts

Kung fu and tai chi embody balance, discipline, and spiritual strength, inspiring practitioners worldwide.

Philosophy

Confucianism, Taoism, and Buddhism continue to influence Chinese values, traditions, and social harmony.



Chinese Cuisine

Discover some of the most iconic and beloved dishes in Chinese culinary tradition.

Peking Duck

Crispy-skinned roast duck served with thin pancakes, scallions, and sweet bean sauce. A Beijing specialty.

Mapo Tofu

A fiery Sichuan dish made with tofu, minced pork, chili bean paste, and numbing Sichuan peppercorns.

Xiaolongbao

Delicate soup dumplings from Shanghai, filled with pork and hot savory broth that bursts with flavor.

Kung Pao Chicken

A stir-fry of diced chicken, peanuts, and chili peppers, balancing savory, sweet, and spicy flavors.




Top Cities to Visit

From ancient capitals to modern skylines, explore China’s most fascinating cities.

Beijing

The capital of China, home to the Forbidden City, Great Wall, and a mix of history and modern culture.

Shanghai

A global financial hub known for its futuristic skyline and the historic Bund waterfront.

Xi’an

China’s ancient capital, famous for the Terracotta Army and Silk Road history.

Chengdu

Home of the giant panda and Sichuan’s world-famous spicy cuisine.
